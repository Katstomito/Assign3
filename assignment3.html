      Risk

      Safety is the first way that our project might harm people. There is a chance that AI robot units, which are still in development, could malfunction and hurt people. For instance, a robot AI unit could set off an explosion that endangers the lives of anyone inside the home. Second, employment is mentioned in our risk assessment because AI robot units may replace human labor, resulting in job loss and hard times financially. For instance, AI robot units could be employed to perform tasks currently carried out by humans, such as cleaning homes, preparing meals, and delivering items. The bias issue is last but not least addressed by the fact that AI robot units are educated using data gathered from the real world. This information may be biased, and the behavior of the AI robot unit may reflect this bias. An AI robot unit, for instance, may be more likely to discriminate against persons of color if it has been taught using data from a predominately white community.

      Group Reflection

      We participated in and were assigned a team project as part of our AI training that was dedicated to developing a robotic unit that can operate indoor systems when given instructions from users, notably humans. In our team's opinion, we are generally rather effective, but there are certainly some areas where we can do better. Being on time and having the ability to work cooperatively are two of the things we accomplish successfully and on schedule throughout the entire project. We were able to make use of the diverse backgrounds and strengths of our teammates. To guarantee that our components operate effortlessly together, we divide the task evenly and according to each person's strengths, as well as assist one another and complete it successfully. In addition, we kept open channels for communication throughout the project, which allowed us to cope with issues as they emerged. On top of that, there were some parts of the project that we could have improved. For instance, we could have done a better job of organizing the aims and objectives for each team member. We all had different duties to complete, yet despite this, we should have been more specific in stating what we were attempting to find and accomplish. In light of this goal, it was difficult at times to determine whether or not we were developing. One thing that surprised me was how fast and how many tasks we were able to accomplish. The project was assigned to us within 4 weeks, and we just took like a day total (24 hours) to complete the whole project. Due to the project’s complexity and the fact that it was completed in less time than expected, we were surprised by the outstanding quality of the final product. In conclusion, the value of delegation and trust is one of the mutual lessons that we learned about teamwork. For our project, we had to have faith that each member of the group would accomplish their allocated assignment. It was crucial to assign jobs to those who had the appropriate skills and expertise. We were able to work well and finish the task on time by having faith in one another and accurately allocating work.
